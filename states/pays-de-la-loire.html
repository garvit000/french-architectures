Château de Saumur

The Château de Saumur is a captivating example of medieval architecture and elegance, set along the picturesque banks of the Loire River. Built in the 10th century and transformed into a stunning Gothic castle in the 14th century, it stands out with its majestic white stone walls, high turrets, and sweeping river views that give it a fairytale-like appearance. Originally a fortress, it has served various purposes throughout history, including as a royal residence and a state prison. Today, it houses a museum showcasing fine arts and equestrian exhibits, allowing visitors to explore its rich heritage and enjoy its breathtaking architectural beauty. The Château de Saumur not only offers historical insights but also mesmerizes with its charming, scenic allure.
