Basilica of Notre-Dame de Fourvière

The Basilica of Notre-Dame de Fourvière in Lyon is a 19th-century church that blends Byzantine and neo-Gothic architectural styles. Built between 1872 and 1884, it was designed to honor the Virgin Mary, who is believed to have protected the city. The basilica's ornate façade is adorned with mosaics and statues, while its interior features colorful mosaics and stained glass. Its central dome and decorative details make it a standout in Lyon's skyline. Today, Notre-Dame de Fourvière is a popular pilgrimage site and tourist attraction. From its hilltop location, visitors enjoy panoramic views of Lyon. Inside, the basilica’s lavish mosaics and intricate frescoes create a stunning atmosphere. The basilica also houses a crypt and a museum, deepening its cultural and religious significance in the city.
